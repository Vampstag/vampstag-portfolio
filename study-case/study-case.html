Tsukamie Noodle Bar

Role Digital Marketing Intern
Duration 3 Months (Aug - Oct 2025)
Scope of Work Content Strategy, Video Production, Influencer Management
Overview

When I joined Tsukamie Noodle Bar, their digital presence was stagnant. TikTok had been inactive for a month, and Instagram engagement was flat. My task was analyzing 1,000+ customer data points to redefine our content pillars and build a sustainable content system.

Within 3 months, we drove +247K TikTok impressions, revitalized Instagram engagement, and managed 20+ local influencers weekly through this new systemized content strategy.

The Challenge
[01]
Platform Inactivity

The TikTok account was dormant and Instagram suffered from stagnant reach, creating a major visibility gap.
[02]
No Structure

There were no content pillars or a consistent calendar, leading to an irregular and ineffective posting schedule.
[03]
Audience Disconnect

Content formats were outdated and misaligned with current F&B social media trends, failing to resonate with the audience.
The Solution
[01]
Data-Driven Strategy

Analyzed 1,000+ customer data points to establish new, relevant content pillars that directly addressed audience preferences.
[02]
Systemized Content

Developed a structured content calendar and production workflow to ensure a consistent schedule of high-quality, trend-aligned video content.
[03]
Community Engagement

Systematically managed collaborations with 20+ local food influencers weekly, amplifying reach and building social proof.
The Impact
Key Takeaways

This internship was a pivotal experience. It was my first time applying classroom theory to a real business where every decision had tangible results. Working with a team, reporting to a business owner, and managing social media for real-world outcomes taught me the importance of structure, data, and consistency. I'm grateful for the opportunity, as it was at Tsukamie that I truly began to build the confidence and practical skills for my marketing career.
